Frank O'Dea Bio

In his early teens, Frank O’Dea’s life went off the rails into a downward spiral. Within a few years, he was living in 50-cent-a-night flop houses in Toronto’s bowery district where just surviving was about all one could do in a day. At 23 years of age, with the courage to hope for a better life, and the spirit to forge ahead, Frank began the long road back. Fighting all of those obstacles that hold back the homeless, the impoverished and the destitute, he overcame and rejoined society. Within a few short years, he co-founded Second Cup, which became the largest chain of gourmet coffees and teas in the country. Committed to giving back, because he experienced the hopeless of poverty first hand, Frank O’Dea has also served on various non-profit boards and either founded or co-founded charitable organizations that focus on poverty awareness. O’Dea’s story of triumph is truly inspiring and instills a sense of hope for those that face poverty on a day-to-day basis.

Manulife Community Builder Award

2013 marks Habitat for Humanity Waterloo Regions (HFHWR) 25th Anniversary. Knowing that this achievement would not have been possible without the support of key individuals, HFHWR has established the Manulife Community Builder award. This award recognizes an individual that has significantly impacted Habitat for Humanity’s success over 25 years and embodies Manulife Financials brand attributes – strong, reliable, trustworthy and forward-thinking.

Manulife Financial has partnered with HFHWR, though House Sponsorship, since 2005. In total, the Manulife crew has built 16 homes with families in need. This relationship is integral part of HFHWR’s success and we are honoured to work with such a revered and philanthropic company.

Address from 25th Anniversary Committee Chair

During the past year I have come to appreciate why Habitat for Humanity Waterloo Region (HFHWR) has been so successful. It’s the people who make Habitat for Humanity what it is.  From the families we partner with, to the sponsors, the staff and the volunteers, HFHWR is made up of many talented and inspiring people. One of the reasons I agreed to lend my energy to these 25th Anniversary celebrations was because of how much I personally admired Milo Shantz, who helped bring Habitat for Humanity to Canada.  With all his many endeavours, Milo was never too busy to help other people and get involved. It has been an honour for me to take part in this significant milestone. I would like to congratulate and thank everyone for supporting HFHWR’s 25th Gala this evening.  Whether you’ve swung a hammer, served a lunch, spent time in our ReStore, or donated your dollars to HFHWR, you have made a positive difference in your community and in the lives of others. Finally, I would also like to thank the following individuals who were part of the 25th Anniversary Committee this past year: Mary Joy Aitken, Vaughn Bender, Melissa Riewald, Jim Erb, Dianne Stickney, Jim Wideman, Karen Redman and Jerry Lawlor. Sincerely, Tania Benninger
